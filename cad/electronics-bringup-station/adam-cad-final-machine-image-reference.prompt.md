# Adam CAD Prompt: Final Machine Enclosure Matching Reference Images

Use this after the internal mechanism assembly is complete.

Create the final production-ready external enclosure for the autonomous electronics bring-up station. The enclosure must visually match the provided front and rear reference images: a compact professional lab automation appliance with a black smoked-glass front, rounded light side shells, black rear service panel, blue-white chamber lighting, and restrained rear I/O.

Wrap the enclosure around the completed internal gantry mechanism without changing the mechanism geometry, fixture origin, gantry travel, camera line of sight, cable-chain clearance, or service volumes.

## Reference Image Targets

Use these visual references:

- Front perspective: black smoked-glass chamber door, rounded light side shells, internal gantry visible, blue-white lighting, slim lower blue status LED, front-right vertical power module.
- Rear view: matte black rear panel, light rounded side shells, horizontal perforated ventilation band, recessed rear-right I/O column, small rear screws, black feet.

## Overall Dimensions

Target external product envelope:

- Width: 750 mm
- Depth: 650 mm
- Height: 700 mm

Maintain these clearances around the internal mechanism:

- Left: approximately 35 mm.
- Right: approximately 35 mm.
- Front: approximately 45 mm for door and glass.
- Rear: approximately 45 mm for I/O, ventilation, and service access.
- Top: 80-100 mm for lighting, overhead camera clearance, and enclosure structure.
- Bottom: 40-60 mm for base plinth and vibration feet.

## Front Industrial Design

Model the front as a sealed premium equipment door.

Required front features:

- Large black smoked tempered-glass viewing window occupying roughly 70 percent of the front face.
- Thick satin-black rounded rectangular front bezel around the glass.
- Single-piece front access door.
- Soft corner radii on the window and bezel.
- Hidden hinges or very clean industrial hinge detail.
- No visible fasteners on the primary front surface.
- No labels, no touchscreen, no knobs, no front display.
- A small vertical rounded power-button module integrated into the front-right edge.
- One subtle illuminated power symbol or small circular power button.
- Thin blue status LED light strip near the lower front edge.
- Black lower plinth/base band.

The internal mechanism should be visible through the smoked glass:

- Horizontal gantry beam.
- Central probe head.
- Drag chain behind the probe head.
- PCB fixture and sample board.
- Cool white or blue-white LED chamber lighting along the upper interior.

## Side and Corner Design

Use the image style:

- Light warm-gray or white rounded side shells on left and right.
- Large vertical corner radii.
- Smooth side panels.
- Subtle panel gaps.
- Precision perforated ventilation patch on the right side if needed.
- No exposed screws on the primary side surfaces except minimal service screws where unavoidable.

Manufacturing interpretation:

- Use aluminum frame with formed sheet metal panels.
- Use molded, machined, or extruded rounded corner covers.
- Keep geometry manufacturable and not overly organic.

## Rear Design

Model the rear as a restrained service panel.

Required rear features:

- Large matte black rear service panel.
- Light rounded side shells wrapping around the rear vertical edges.
- Horizontal perforated ventilation band across the lower-middle rear panel.
- Recessed vertical I/O column on the rear-right side.
- Small rear service screws around the panel perimeter.
- Four black vibration-isolation feet visible at the bottom.

I/O column must include only:

- One RJ45 Ethernet port.
- One USB-C service port.
- One IEC AC inlet.
- One rocker power switch.

Do not add additional connectors.

## Internal Chamber Details

Add chamber finishing around the existing mechanism:

- Dark interior walls.
- Concealed upper LED light strips.
- Black inner frame surfaces.
- Clear optical line of sight through front glass to the PCB fixture.
- No exposed loose wiring.
- No visible benchtop instruments.

Do not change:

- X travel: 500 mm.
- Y travel: 400 mm.
- Z travel: 100 mm.
- PCB fixture location.
- Probe head clearance.
- Overhead camera line of sight.
- Cable chain motion envelopes.

## Base and Feet

Create a black lower base/plinth:

- Slightly recessed below front door.
- Thin blue LED status strip integrated near the front lower edge.
- Four black industrial vibration-isolation feet.
- Feet should appear robust enough for lab automation equipment.

## Materials

Apply these visual materials:

- Front bezel and inner chamber: satin black anodized aluminum or black powder coat.
- Smoked window: dark translucent glass or tinted polycarbonate.
- Side shells/corner covers: light warm-gray powder coat or molded polymer.
- Rear panel: matte black powder-coated sheet metal.
- Fixture: bead-blasted aluminum.
- Internal gantry: dark gray anodized aluminum.
- Probe head: matte black machined aluminum.
- Status lighting: subtle blue.
- Chamber lighting: cool white to blue-white.
- Feet: black rubber over metal mounts.

## Manufacturing Requirements

The model must look production-ready:

- Sheet metal panels suitable for laser cutting and bending.
- Aluminum structural frame.
- Molded, machined, or extruded rounded corner covers.
- Realistic bend radii.
- Realistic panel gaps.
- Removable rear and side service panels.
- Rear screws only where serviceable.
- Hidden front fasteners.
- Filtered ventilation section.
- No sci-fi styling.
- No impossible one-piece monolithic shell.

## Output Subassemblies

Create or organize the final enclosure into:

- Front door and smoked glass.
- Black front bezel.
- Left rounded side shell.
- Right rounded side shell.
- Rear service panel.
- Rear I/O column.
- Ventilation filter/perforation panel.
- Top cover.
- Lower base plinth.
- Feet.
- Chamber LED strips.
- Enclosure mounting brackets to internal frame.

The finished CAD should resemble a real product ready for industrial design review and mechanical engineering refinement.
