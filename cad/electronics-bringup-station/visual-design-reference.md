## Final Machine Character

The final machine should look like a compact professional laboratory automation appliance, not a generic CNC router or 3D printer.

Key visual traits:

- Black smoked-glass front door.
- Deep black front bezel with large rounded rectangular window.
- White or light warm-gray rounded side shells.
- Matte black rear service panel.
- Blue-white internal chamber lighting.
- Thin blue status light strip near the lower front edge.
- Small vertical power button module on the front-right edge.
- Rear recessed I/O column with only Ethernet, USB-C, IEC AC inlet, and power switch.
- Horizontal perforated rear ventilation band.
- Small black vibration-isolation feet.

## Front View Requirements

The front should be dominated by the viewing chamber.

Approximate proportions:

- Viewing window: 70 percent of front face width.
- Black bezel: thick enough to feel like a sealed industrial door, not a thin picture frame.
- Side shells: visible rounded white/light-gray vertical corner pieces.
- Lower base: black horizontal plinth with a slim blue LED status line.
- Front-right control: one vertical rounded black module containing power button and small indicator light.

The internal mechanism should be visible through the glass:

- Horizontal gantry rail/crossbeam.
- Central probe head.
- Drag chain behind or beside probe head.
- PCB fixture centered below.
- Blue-white LED strips along upper interior.

Do not add:

- Touchscreen.
- Keypad.
- Labels on the front.
- Knobs.
- Exposed test equipment.
- Exposed loose wires.

## Rear View Requirements

The rear should look serviceable and restrained.

Required rear features:

- Large matte black rear service panel.
- Light rounded side shell corners wrapping around left and right edges.
- Horizontal perforated ventilation band across lower-middle rear area.
- Recessed vertical I/O panel on rear-right side.
- I/O panel includes:
  - Ethernet jack.
  - USB-C service port.
  - IEC AC inlet.
  - Rocker power switch.
- Small screw heads only on rear service panel and rear I/O module.
- Four small vibration feet visible below.

Avoid:

- Excess ports.
- Fan grilles that look like PC cases.
- Consumer labels or logos.
- Bright colors beyond subtle blue status lighting.

## Materials

Suggested visual materials:

- Front bezel: satin black anodized aluminum or painted formed metal.
- Viewing window: smoked tempered glass or tinted polycarbonate.
- Side shells/corners: light warm-gray powder-coated aluminum or molded corner covers.
- Rear panel: matte black powder-coated sheet metal.
- Fixture plate: bead-blasted aluminum.
- Internal gantry: dark gray anodized aluminum with black carriage hardware.
- Probe head: matte black or dark gray machined aluminum.
- Lighting: cool white to blue-white LED strips.

## Enclosure Geometry Notes

The visual target has soft product-level corners but should still be manufacturable.

Use:

- Bent sheet metal panels.
- Rounded corner covers or corner extrusions.
- Large-radius vertical side shells.
- Realistic panel gaps.
- Rear panel screws placed on serviceable edges.
- Hidden front fasteners.

Do not use:

- Organic sci-fi shells.
- Impossible seamless monolithic bodies.
- Overly complex surfacing.
- Thin consumer-electronics proportions.

## Design Priority

The final enclosure should preserve the internal mechanism envelope first, then wrap the industrial design around it. The gantry, fixture, probe head, cable chains, cameras, rear service volume, and ventilation paths should remain believable and serviceable.
