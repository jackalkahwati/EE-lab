FIRSTLIGHT FL-1

Autonomous PCB bring-up & diagnosis station — every board's first light

What it is

FirstLight FL-1 takes a never-powered board straight from the fab and autonomously answers "does it work — and if not, why not." Fixture the board, load its manifest, close the glass, walk away. The machine runs vision registration, a pre-power safety gate, sequenced power-up, firmware programming, full-board probing, and thermal inspection — then renders a diagnosis, not a guess: which test failed, where, with measurements and imagery attached.

It replaces the first days of bench bring-up — the careful, repetitive, easy-to-get-wrong part — with a repeatable autonomous run operated entirely from the front touch panel or the web UI.

Core capabilities

  • Pre-power safety gate — resistance-to-ground, diode-mode, isolation and live-voltage checks before any power touches the DUT.
  • 3-axis precision probing — force-compliant probe head with a 3-station tool-changing cartridge rack: pogo cluster, Kelvin 4-wire, and 200 MHz shielded scope tip, each with calibration EEPROM.
  • Power instrumentation — sequenced multi-rail DUT power, nA-to-A inrush/leakage capture, programmable electronic load.
  • Measurement matrix — scope, 16-ch logic, DAQ and 6.5-digit DMM routed to any probe through a protected, self-calibrating relay matrix (custom PCBA, <100 pA leakage paths).
  • Firmware & interfaces — SWD/JTAG and SPI-flash programming, UART/I²C/SPI bridging, USB/Ethernet/CAN-FD connector-level test panel.
  • Vision + thermal — machine-vision registration and inspection, probe-tip camera, and FLIR thermal anomaly localization in a still-air chamber.
